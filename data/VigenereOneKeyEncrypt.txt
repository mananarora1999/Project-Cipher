The intercepted message,which was sent as he signed off on 7 May 1945 at 7.35am,
said: 'British troops entered Cuxhaven at 1400 on 6 May - from now on all radio
traffic will cease - wishing you all the best. Lt Kunkel'.This was immediately followed
by: 'Closing down for ever - all the best - goodbye'.While most of the UK was preparing to
celebrate the war ending, and the last of the German military communicators surrendered,
Bletchley staff - like today's GCHQ workers - carried on working to help keep the country safe.
